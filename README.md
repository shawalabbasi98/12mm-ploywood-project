# 12 mm Plywood Structural / Storage Rack Design

## Project Overview
This project focuses on the design and development of a **structural storage rack made from 12 mm plywood** for an Australian client. The goal was to create a lightweight, durable, and easy-to-assemble structure optimized for **CNC manufacturing** while minimizing material waste.

The design uses **slot-fit / interlocking joints**, allowing assembly without screws or adhesives.

---

## Material
- **Material:** Plywood
- **Thickness:** 12 mm
- **Manufacturing Method:** CNC Router / Laser Cutting

---

## Design Work
- 3D CAD modeling of all plywood components using **SolidWorks**
- Preparation of **2D manufacturing drawings** using SolidWorks and AutoCAD
- Development of **slot-fit / interlocking joints** for tool-free assembly
- Proper **dimensioning and tolerance setup** for CNC cutting
- Structural layout planning to distribute loads efficiently

---

## Manufacturing Considerations
- Design optimized for **CNC router / laser cutting**
- **Material nesting** to reduce plywood waste
- Proper **clearance tolerances** for smooth part fitting
- Structural reinforcement through geometric design

---

## Deliverables
- Complete **3D assembly model**
- Detailed **2D part drawings**
- **CNC-ready DXF / DWG files**
- Assembly-ready component layout

---

## Problems & Design Challenges
- **Material Limitation:** Designing a structurally stable rack using only 12 mm plywood required careful load distribution to prevent bending.
- **Joint Strength:** Developing slot-fit / interlocking joints strong enough for assembly without screws or adhesives.
- **Tolerance Issues:** Achieving precise CNC cutting tolerances so parts fit properly during assembly.
- **Material Optimization:** Minimizing plywood waste through efficient sheet nesting.
- **Structural Stability:** Ensuring the final structure remains rigid and stable under load.

---

## Assembly Guide
1. Arrange all CNC-cut plywood parts according to the assembly layout.
2. Insert interlocking panels into the designed slot joints.
3. Ensure all components are properly aligned before final fitting.
4. Apply slight pressure to secure tight-fitting joints.
5. Verify the structure for stability and alignment.
